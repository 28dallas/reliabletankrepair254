7 Signs Your Water Tank Needs Repair

Learn the warning signs that indicate your water tank requires professional attention

Water tanks are essential for storing clean water in Kenyan homes and businesses. However, like any infrastructure, they can develop problems over time. Recognizing the early signs of tank damage can save you from costly repairs and water shortages. Here are the 7 key signs that your water tank needs repair.

1. Visible Leaks and Drips

One of the most obvious signs is water pooling around the tank base or visible drips. Even small leaks can waste significant amounts of water and indicate damage to the tank's structure. In Kenya's climate, temperature changes can cause plastic tanks to expand and contract, leading to cracks and leaks.

2. Decreased Water Pressure

If you're noticing reduced water flow from your taps, it could indicate a leak in your water tank or supply lines. This is especially common in areas with high water demand like Nairobi and its suburbs. Check your water meter for unexplained consumption increases.

3. Strange Tastes or Odors

A properly maintained water tank should not affect water quality. If your water tastes or smells unusual, it could indicate contamination from a crack, algae growth, or sediment buildup. This is particularly important for health and requires immediate attention.

4. Discolored Water

Rust-colored water often indicates corrosion in steel tanks or sediment accumulation. For plastic tanks, discoloration might suggest algae growth or material degradation. Both conditions require professional inspection and repair.

5. Visible Cracks or Damage

Physical damage to your tank is a clear indicator. Look for cracks, holes, bulges, or warping on both the interior and exterior surfaces. In Kenya, UV exposure can accelerate plastic degradation, especially in tanks installed in direct sunlight.

6. Increased Water Bills

Unexplained increases in your water bill are often the first financial indicators of a hidden leak. Even small leaks can waste thousands of liters over time. Regular monitoring of your water consumption can help detect problems early.

7. Noisy Tank Operations

Unusual sounds like banging, whistling, or gurgling from your water tank system can indicate air leaks, loose fittings, or internal damage. These sounds should not be ignored as they often precede more serious problems.

What to Do If You Notice These Signs

If you observe any of these warning signs, it's crucial to contact professional water tank repair services immediately.
